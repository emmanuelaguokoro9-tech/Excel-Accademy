Student FAQs

Quick answers to common questions about academics, campus life, policies, and more

Academics & Curriculum

What subjects are offered in JSS?

JSS students study: Mathematics, English, Basic Science, Social Studies, Business Studies, Computer Studies, French, Cultural & Creative Arts, Physical & Health Education, Civic Education, and Agricultural Science.

What are the science department subjects?

Science students take: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics, Further Mathematics, English, Computer Science, and Agricultural Science.

How are continuous assessments calculated?

Continuous Assessment (CA) makes up 40% of your final grade. It includes class tests, assignments, projects, class participation, and homework.

What is the promotion policy?

Students must achieve a minimum 50% average to be promoted. SS3 students must meet WAEC registration requirements.

Is there extra help for struggling students?

Yes! We offer after-school tutorials, peer tutoring programs, and teachers are available during break time for extra assistance.

Campus Life

What are school hours?

School runs from 8:00 AM to 3:00 PM, Monday through Friday. Morning assembly starts at 7:45 AM. Late arrival after 8:00 AM is recorded.

Is there a cafeteria? What food is served?

Yes! Our modern cafeteria serves nutritious breakfast, lunch, and snacks. Options include rice, beans, yam, eggs, chicken, fruits, and healthy beverages.

How do I report a lost item?

Visit the school's Lost & Found office located near the main reception. Label all belongings with your name for easy identification.

What medical facilities are available?

We have a fully equipped clinic with a registered nurse on-site during school hours. Emergency care and first aid are always available.

School Policies

What is the uniform dress code?

Boys: White shirt, navy blue trousers, blue tie, black shoes. Girls: White blouse, navy blue pinafore/skirt, blue tie, black shoes. P.E.: Navy blue track suit and white sneakers.

Are mobile phones allowed?

No. Mobile phones and smartwatches are strictly prohibited during school hours. They will be confiscated and returned only to parents.

What is the policy on bullying?

Excel Academy has ZERO tolerance for bullying. Report any incidents to your class teacher, counselor, or use the anonymous reporting box. Disciplinary action will be taken.

How do I request a leave of absence?

Submit a written request from your parent/guardian at least 24 hours in advance. Emergency leave requires a phone call to the school office before 8:00 AM.

What happens if I miss an examination?

A valid medical certificate or documented emergency is required for a make-up exam. Unexcused absences result in a zero grade.

Sports & Clubs

What sports are available?

Football, basketball, swimming, athletics (track & field), table tennis, volleyball, and chess. Inter-house sports competitions are held annually.

How do I join a club?

Club registration happens at the beginning of each term. Listen to announcements for sign-up dates or speak to the club advisor.

What clubs are offered?

Debate Club, Science Club, Press Club, Robotics Club, Coding Club, Drama Club, Choir, Art Club, Red Cross, and Cultural Dance Club.

Can I start a new club?

Yes! Submit a proposal to the Student Affairs office with a mission statement, faculty advisor, and at least 10 interested members.

Technology & ICT

Do we have computer labs?

Yes! Two modern computer labs with 50+ computers, high-speed internet, and the latest software for learning.

Can I use my laptop in class?

Personal laptops are allowed for senior students only with teacher permission. All use must be academic-related.

What coding languages do we learn?

Our ICT curriculum covers Python, HTML/CSS, JavaScript, Scratch, and robotics programming depending on the class level.

Is Wi-Fi available for students?

Wi-Fi is available in computer labs and designated areas for educational purposes only. Personal use is restricted.
